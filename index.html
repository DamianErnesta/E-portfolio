About Me

I am 23 years old this year and in my final year of my Electrical and Electronic Engineering Degree at Asia Pacific University of Technology and Innovation (APU). I am on my last semester now, eager to learn more about power systems, optimization algorithms and embedded system design. My goal is to be a well-trained, reliable engineer who can apply engineering sciences to making ultimate solutions for real-life engineering problems.


Final Year Project: Optimal Capacitor Placement and Sizing in a Radial Distribution System using Zebra Optimization Algorithm (ZOA) with power loss minimization and voltage profile improvement. Additionally, my contribution to a team design project using Raspberry Pi, sensors, and computer vision techniques was a pair of AI smart glasses that help visually impaired people navigate and detect objects in their surroundings.


Previously, I did a four-month internship at Public Utilities Corporation (PUC) in Seychelles, where I was exposed to several sectors such as distribution, underground cable systems and renewable energies. It collectively strengthened my understanding of real world engineering standards and deepened my sense of ongoing self improvement and innovation.
