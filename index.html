About Me

Mission Statement

My dream is to be an electrical engineer capable of dealing with the new concepts, serving honestly, and using sustainable practices to solve the difficult engineering problems. It is my desire to actually contribute in the society through constant learning and personal development as an individual and as a professional.

I'm Damian Etienne Ernesta, a fourth year Electrical and Electronics Engineering student at Asia Pacific University. Through the work that I did, it has included working in real life technical projects, innovation through projects, and a high level of exposure to real engineering.

In my Final Year Project, I was looking at the optimal placement and sizing of capacitors within a radial distribution system using the Zebra Optimization Algorithm (ZOA) where I coded and tested the algorithm in MATLAB, as well as compared it with other methods to minimize power loss and optimize the system.

With this, I also participated in a group project where we develop a AI based smart glasses to help visually impaired people where we used a Raspberry Pi 5, AI camera, ultrasonic sensors, and GPS modules to enable real time navigation giving feedbacks on obstacles and voice assistance.

My engineering internship was in the Public Utilities Corporation (PUC) in Seychelles where I got the opportunity to rotate through various departments such as the Inspectorate, Planning, Distribution, Power Station Operation & Maintenance, Underground Cabling Projects, Wind Turbines, and Ile de Romainville Solar Farm. This provided me with an important experience in working in the field of electrical systems, maintenance procedures, and sustainable energy operations.

Now I have interest in power system analysis, Python and embedded systems like ESP32 and Raspberry Pi. I am also enhancing my technical skills and problem solving abilities all the time. After graduation, I would like to work professionally as an Electrical and Electronics Engineer to help in the design of efficient, intelligent and sustainable systems and their maintenance.

SWOT Analysis

Taking a look at my school practice and practical experiences, I realize what I am good at. My top strengths include my high grades and my outstanding technical skills in MATLAB, optimization and embedded systems. I possess practical experience as well through internships and projects and I am a team player and can communicate effectively.

I do know some also weaknesses that I should to work on. I have to improve my ability to analyze problems and to keep learning independently. I must also learn to tackle problems better when I am under pressure, should study more on my own and learn to write down the work, rather than just concentrate on the technical aspects.

In the future, I can observe numerous opportunities to develop. My final projects can assist me to secure employment after I graduate. I also get a chance to study online courses so that I can improve on my skills. I might want to specialize in power systems or IoT systems and get to know more people in my industry at industry events, on LinkedIn, and in groups such as IEEE.

There are also threats that I am noticing that I should be prepared. Technology is something that evolves very fast and I cannot afford not to learn. The new engineers job market is stiff. Without continuous self-education, I can fall behind, and I should be cautious of my time management to be able to complete my work on time, especially in stressful scenarios.
